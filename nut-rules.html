NUT Rules — WOS State 2128

We don’t do NAP — we do NUT: No Unnecessary Trouble.

Welcome to the NUT page, where alliances collaborate to maintain harmony and competitiveness in State 2128. If your alliance chooses not to participate, you may opt out. By default, all alliances are opted in.

NUT Rules

Freedom of Speech

  • No restrictions on speech in world or private chats.
  • If someone offends you, use the blacklist feature to block them.

City Attacks

  • Furnace level 20 and below can attack anyone anytime.
  • Do not repeatedly attack the same city to zero them.
  • Scouting: Allowed at all times.

Alliance Structures

  • Alliance headquarters cannot be attacked.
  • Alliance banners cannot be attacked unless the banners block access to facilities.
  • If banners block access: contact the R5 and R4 before attacking and allow them time to move banners (shouldn’t take more than 24 hours).

Tile Attacks

  • Allowed only during Kill Event (KE).
  • If a player gathers in another alliance’s territory, those tiles can be attacked.

Scheduled Battles

  • Alliances can schedule fights by mutual agreement.
  • No attacking outside of the scheduled time.

Presidency Rotation

  • Rotates among the top 10 alliances in NUT.
  • “Whales” should switch alliances to help hold the castle.
  • No rallies on the Sunfire Castle (solo attacks only) to make it easier for any alliance to hold it.

Whale Distribution

  • “Whales” should spread out during major events (e.g., Castle Battle) to create an equalized battle.

Fortress / Stronghold Distribution

  • Rotates among alliances with at least 50 active members.
  • Alliances can trade fortresses/strongholds based on reward preferences.
  • Any alliance not assigned a fortress/stronghold can attack any fortress/stronghold of their choice.

State vs State (SvS)

  • Players can switch alliances to help in SvS.
  • Players should return to their original alliance after unless they choose to stay and there is space.
  • R5s will share the SvS plan with their alliance. Follow the plan—don’t be an embarrassment.

Recruitment

  • World chat and private message recruitment is always allowed.
  • If members leave, it’s a leadership/alliance issue—not a recruitment problem.
  • No retaliation against any person/alliance that recruits from another.

Facilities

  • All facilities are free-for-all.
  • No blocking alliances from “touching” facilities.
  • No alliance should control more than one of the same facility type at the same time. (e.g., two Lv1 Tech Facilities is pointless; buffs don’t stack. Share it—don’t be stupid.)

Reminder: NUT is a social contract. If your alliance opts out, you’re not protected by these rules and others may engage freely.

Brothers in Arms Rules

Brothers in Arms is a high-intensity, two-day PvP event in Whiteout Survival where alliances battle for dominance by attacking enemy cities and troops. Players earn points by killing or severely injuring troops, with rewards based on individual and alliance rankings. Strategic coordination is key, as alliances must balance offense and defense while protecting their own resources and structures.

  • Shield or Fight: If you don’t want to participate, shield up. No complaints. Stay in alliance territory for higher safety.
  • City Attacks: Max 2 attacks per city. If a city is burning, someone already hit twice—move on.
  • Alliance Structures: HQs and banners are off-limits.
  • Tile Combat: Resource tiles are fair game; gather at your own risk.
  • No Facility/Bear Interference: Don’t attack an alliance during facility battles or bear fights.
  • Team Strategy Allowed: Organized group attacks are fine within attack limits.
  • Respect Weaker Players: Only attack within ±5 Furnace levels.

Opt-Out Information

If your alliance chooses to opt out of NUT, notify the leadership council. Opting out means your alliance will not be protected by NUT rules, and other alliances may engage freely with your members. Ensure this decision aligns with your alliance’s goals and strategy.
